RH Freeman — Logo Concepts

Eight directions exploring how the brand could evolve while staying true to its heritage

Prepared by Data Tradies
Heritage Direction
Concepts 1–3: Established, refined, built to last
These lean into Freeman's 78-year history. Serif typography and restrained layouts that say "we've been doing this longer than anyone."
1. Classic Serif Lockup
Cormorant Garamond headings with the existing RHF monogram. Vertical gold divider creates a traditional heraldic structure. The tagline grounds the brand in what they do.
HeritageConcept A
2. Stacked Heritage
Centred, symmetrical, almost like a wine label or premium brand stamp. The horizontal gold rules frame the name with quiet authority. Strong on letterheads and signage.
HeritageSignage
3. Monogram & Wordmark
The monogram splits RH/F across a gold divider inside a navy block — creating a crest-like mark. Works at any size: the monogram alone on uniforms, favicon; full lockup on the website.

Industrial Direction
Concepts 4–5: Bold, direct, factory-floor confidence
Oversized display type, sharp edges, zero decoration. These say "we build things" without apology.
4. Industrial Block
Gold mark reversed into Bebas Neue display type. The thick gold underline is structural — like a steel beam. Monospace tagline with bullet separator. No subtlety, all confidence.
IndustrialConcept B
5. Bold Sans-Serif
Archivo Black at max weight — a heavyweight industrial sans-serif. "FREEMAN" dominates, RH sits quietly above in gold. The gold accent bar is a subtle nod to raw material. Clean, powerful, modern.

Contemporary Direction
Concepts 6–8: Clean, approachable, modern professional
Rounded forms, balanced weight, warm tones. These position Freeman as progressive and easy to work with while maintaining credibility.
6. Rounded Contemporary
Outfit typeface is geometric and modern but warm. The rounded square mark (radius-10) feels approachable — more tech company than traditional fabrication. Mixed case "Freeman" softens the brand.
ContemporaryConcept C
7. Pill Badge
Everything contained in a pill-shaped border — mirrors the floating nav in Concept C. The gold circle mark with dark initials creates a coin/badge effect. Feels cohesive and contained.
ContemporaryContained
8. Minimal Wordmark
No icon, no mark — just the name does the work. Lowercase "rh" in navy, "freeman" in gold. The weight difference and colour split create the visual structure. Ultra-clean, ultra-modern. Monospace tagline anchors it.

Notes for Discussion

These are direction indicators, not final designs. Once a direction is chosen, the logo would be refined in detail — kerning, weight, proportions, and file formats for print, web, and embroidery.

The monogram matters. Every concept includes a standalone mark (the square/circle with RHF) that works as a favicon, hard hat sticker, uniform embroidery, or social media avatar.

Heritage vs. Modern isn't binary. The most effective approach might combine elements — say Concept 3's crest-style monogram with Concept 6's clean Outfit typeface. The goal is to find the right balance between "we've been here since 1946" and "we invest in modern capability."

The gold is doing a lot of work. It's what separates these from a generic engineering brand. Keeping it as the primary accent (rather than a secondary colour) is worth protecting in any final direction.
